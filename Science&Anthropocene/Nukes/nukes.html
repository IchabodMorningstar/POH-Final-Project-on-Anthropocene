How humans utilise nukes in the Anthropocene

Millitary Weapons

Only nine countries on Earth legally possess nuclear weapons. There're mainly three types of nuclear weapons, known as Fusion, Fission and Neutron Bombs.

Power Plants

Nuclear power is a clean energy, whose wastes won't contaminate the environment after proper hanlde. It uses the fission reaction like A-bomb.

Potential Technologies

Potential nuclear technologies include medical use for cancer detecting and healing, pesticides alternatives, transportation power sourse and in more fields.

Interesting News

In 2022, many European countries announce ceasing the use of nuclear power plants (NPP). Environmental protection is a responsibility on humans' shoulders, but nuclear power are clean energy which, if handled properly, is friendly to the eco-system. What some so-called environmentalists boycott is their fear of nuke, especially after the catastrophe in Chernobyl and the irresponsible disposal in Fukushima.

If humans manage to target the mutated DNA, then nukes bring new brightness to the welfare of humans.

The Anthropocene is essentially an era of humans, so the usages of nukes are affected by economy, politics, technologies and the like, as proposed by Gregory Ferguson-Cradler in "Corporate Strategy in the Anthropocene." In 2011, German major power production utilities faced a survival crisis of sudden changes in nuclear power regulation, as renewable energy competition intensified, and the economy declined. Moreover, nukes are a military strategic resource, the world is afraid of improper use as weapons instead of welfare providers, hence the popularization of nuclear power globally will be hindered by the world's major powers, for instance, NATO intervenes with nukes in Iran. On physics level, humans haven't acquired enough knowledge of nuclear reactions, thus our exploitation of nukes is just like the ancients playing with fire.

More Details on Threats of Nukes

Anthropocene and Nukes

Nukes were almost irrelevant to humans until the Anthropocene began. Nukes are so powerful that humans cannot fully control. Powerful things tend to be a double-edged blade, and so are nukes. With nuclear reaction, Anthropocene cities can be wiped out in seconds, while potential space transportation can travel kilometers in seconds. The impacts of nukes on the Anthropocene are evident, as they mark the beginning and illuminate the future. The nuclear power shown on this page is a great aspect of nukes in the Anthropocene. It brought catastrophes in Hiroshima and Chernobyl, yet humans can still count on it to ease the environmental pollutions. We, humans, should take the positive advantages of nukes and proceed into the Anthropocene with the benefits nukes offer to us.
