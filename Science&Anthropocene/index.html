Science in Practice and in Cinema

Many aspects of science were less influentical to the human society until the Anthropocene commences. Let's explore with Nukes (Physics), Pesticides (Biology), Fertilizer (Chemistry) and Motion Pictures (Social Entertainment)!

Start Exploring ↓ Motion Pictures

Project Introduction

Science has its dichotomy of benefit and destruction since humans utilized it in reality. Most scientists carried the will of benefiting the world and invented novel objects. Meanwhile, their inventions were taken the advantage of by those who posed their ambition over the general good. The Anthropocene, in which many conditions and processes on Earth are profoundly altered by human impact, divides the effects of some inventions that had little influence on humans until this era begins. Our group tries to use the motion pictures as a thread to connect nukes, pesticides and fertilizers, and in doing so we will demonstrate why they can represent these influential and double-edged inventions in the Anthropocene.

Topics Introduction

Explore science in the Anthropocene from 4 domains

01

Nukes

Nuclear usage was a novel invention for humans in the 20th century and its fallout marks the commencement of the Anthropocene to some extent. The international society witnessed its power for the first in Hiroshima, the A-bomb “little boy”. Meanwhile, nukes can be a cure for environmental pollution and currently unsolvable diseases. We wish for a better future based on benign nuclear usage and compensate for the catastrophes it has already brought to us.

02

Pesticides

Pesticides were originally used to combat insect vectors for disease during World War II. After being adapted for the control of insect pests in agriculture, pesticides effectively shrink the population of pests and become one of the symbols of agriculture in the Anthropocene. However, the heavy use of pesticides has allowed them to reach most parts of the Earth through the water cycle, which had implicit but severe negative impacts on other non-target organisms, including humans, in various ways.

03

Fertilizers

Fertilizer, a major agricultural invention in the Anthropocene, is driving the development of agriculture while changing the environment. The fertilizer consumption has increased rapidly in decades and has become a significant part of the Great Acceleration of the Anthropocene. Fertilizer is often very helpful for crop short-term agricultural planting. However, since the development of synthetic fertilizers, various nitrogen fertilizer have gradually impacted the cycle of these chemical elements in the eco-system, and crises arise accordingly.

04

Motion Pictures

Motion picture, an Anthropocene invention combining visualization, sound effects and storytelling, records humans and their inventions' huge impact on the world.And Jennifer Peterson and Graig Uhlin summarize cinema's potential in this regard: "The Anthropocene ... puts an end to any separation of natural history from human history, and if there is any representational medium that best captures natural environments made human, it is cinema." Furthermore, its representation and imagination of the future reflects and impacts contemporary people's thoughts and behavior.

Thread: How Topics Connect

Science in the Cinema

The invention of nukes, fertilizers and pesticides were initially to help humans to gain more energy and food. Though they did achieve their inventors' targets in some respects, their side impacts on the environment are also significant. As film theorist Jennifer Fay argues in her book "Inhospitable World: Cinema in the Time of the Anthropocene":

“Our collective efforts to make the planet more welcoming, secure and productive for human flourishing, especially in the twentieth and twenty- first centuries, are precisely the means that have made this a less hospitable earth, transforming it from something given into a disaster we have made”

Blade Runner series, as one of the most far-reaching science film series, innovatively depicts an “inhospitable” future world caused by technologies including the other three topics this project covers.

Motion Pictures

Motion Pictures in Anthropocene: How Blade Runner and Blade Runner 2049 Constructs and Represents the Apocalyptic Future World

Plot and worldbuilding

The story happens mainly in Los Angeles in 2019 and 2049, the artificial disasters in Anthropocene including mass distinction, climate change and environmental collapse has made the Earth desert and noir. Big companies which control technologies of replicant people and synthesis agriculture dominate the world, a world which can be summarized as “high tech, low life”. Though people can travel in air vehicles and explore interstellar planets, their food is made of worms raised in a synthesis system, their loneliness can only be consoled by an artificial girlfriend. The two films center around the replicant people(regarded as slaves and machines by humans), whose souls are completely same as humans, find themselves in this inhospitable manmade world.

Environment and Cinematography

Soundtrack

The origin motion picture soundtrack is composed by progressive Greek musician Vangelis, who employs electronic and synthetic music to introduce the future world. Through the resonant electronic sound, the exotic and hazy dystopian atmosphere is vividly depicted. The synthesizer, harbinger of a strange future of simulated sound, emphasizes the artificial division we're witnessing in which only the (apparently Caucasian) elite have the right to see the sun or to see untainted beauty. Besides, traditional instruments including piano and saxophone are adopted in the romantic and sentimental scenes.

Anthropocene in Blade Runner series

The presentation of the Anthropocene in Blade Runner series is abstract and in the position of background. As director Sarah Hamblin and actor Hugh C. O'Connell argue,

“Yet one of the most interesting things about reading 2049 as Anthropocene film is that it is decidedly not about the Anthropocene in any specific sense…2049 incorporates the Anthropocene into its worldbuilding as an inescapable aspect of its near-future imaginary.”

The impact of the Anthropocene is so comprehensive and significant that it cannot only be presented in climate change, or technologies like nukes, fertilizers or pesticides. Instead, we should comprehend it as a new geological epoch which influences every aspect in our life. Though individuals' existence in it may be like “tears in rain”, we should protect our planet to avoid the dystopian future in Blade Runner series.
