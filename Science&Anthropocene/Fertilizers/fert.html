Fertilizer in the Anthropocene

With the world's increasing population, agricultural productivity development has become necessary. In order to improve productivity, mankind has invented a series of scientific and technological means, such as fertilizers, to transform the earth.

Fertilizer, its benefits, and the great acceleration

Jon Fasman mentioned in "The new Anthropocene diet" that "depleted soils are chemically tarted up into a fecund semblance of health with nutrients straight from the factory." Indeed, fertilizer, especially chemical fertilizer, has the astonishing effect of changing soil composition and making crops grow healthily. Because of the powerful ability of fertilizer, it is used worldwide and has become a part of the great acceleration, which has an important influence in the Anthropocene.

Essential fertilizers and their pros

The three most essential fertilizers are nitrogen fertilizer, phosphorus fertilizer, and potassium fertilizer. Those components in them play significant roles in plant growth.

But fertilizer can't be perfect, can it? Moreover, can we use fertilizer without restraint?

From Shuangfeng, Hunan, China...

A photo of Shuangfeng, Hunan province, from Sohu.com

Farmers closer to agriculture may know more about it, so I interviewed my mother, Caixia Li. She was born in 1975 in Shuangfeng, Hunan province, China. Her youth age, the 1980s and 1990s, was a period of rapid development of China's agriculture industry. Growing up in the countryside, she witnessed the development of China's agriculture, various changes in the use of fertilizers, and improvements in people's ideas about fertilizers.

She mentions that fertilizer was a rush-to-buy commodity at that time: "The rural supply and marketing cooperatives had special sales of fertilizer like ammonia, urea, phosphate fertilizer, nitrogen fertilizer, etc., which was transported to the supply and marketing cooperatives truck by truck." "There was a time when you had to buy fertilizer with tickets." She adds. At the same time, she also saw the harm of chemical fertilizers: "Sometimes after using chemical fertilizers and pesticides, all the loaches in the fields died... In just over a decade, the ponds and rivers in the countryside were polluted, the water quality was no longer clear, and there were no loaches or small fish in the rice fields anymore."

What happened in Hunan rural areas is not an individual case.

To the whole world

All over the world, the massive use of fertilizers has had an immeasurable impact on the environment. In addition, the large-scale use of chemical fertilizers worldwide will affect the circulation of chemical elements in the earth's system.

An animation of the nitrogen cycle process, made by me

Take nitrogen fertilizer as an example. The above animation shows the nitrogen cycle process, in which nitrogen fertilizer usually brings its power to provide NH3 and NH4. In the case of excessive use, a significant number of NH3 and NH4 will be converted into NO2. Emma Bryce, a journalist, says in her article "The Fertilizer Solution Has Become A Major Climate Problem" that "at 300 times the potential of carbon dioxide, and persisting for 100 years in the atmosphere, this gas has a found shaping effect on climate change". In the long run, the massive use of nitrogen fertilizer harms the environment and human beings.

Possible harms caused by nitrogen fertilizer

Fortunately, human beings have gradually understood the downside of these invented technologies.

Turning point

China is the top user of nitrogen-based fertilizers. However, farmers gradually have a deeper understanding of fertilizer and learned the harm of overuse. "A small amount of compound fertilizer and urea is still used in rural areas. People who rent land in cities will not use chemical fertilizers and pesticides because they want to eat healthy green vegetables. " Caixia Li mentions in the interview.

A photo of the city farm in Beijing suburbs, taken by me

The International Nitrogen Management System, a five-year, $60 million research project from the UN Environment Program and the Global Environment Facility, organized a seminar. They specifically pointed out the existence of the Nitrogen Pollution Crisis and called on to reduce the use of nitrogen fertilizer.

Our tomorrow

We still have no answer to what the Anthropocene's future will be. But we have come a long way trying to find it. Fertilizers, as well as other scientific and technological means invented by human beings to improve our lives, require us to understand their pros and cons and evaluate their short-term and long-term impacts. Only by understanding science and using it cautiously can human beings go forward in the future.
